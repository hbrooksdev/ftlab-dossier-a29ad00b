The determination · roles

How Many Roles? Derived From the Work

Re-running the count bottom-up from FTLAB’s actual activities and the founders’ own words — not from AI-born theory. The answer is ~3 Engines, not seven.

6 min readClassification · Strategic / InternalSource · ftlab-role-derivation-from-work-v0.1.md
In brief

Derives FTLAB’s functional roles from the business plan and the founders’ open-question answers. Two stated intentions collapse the work: “the ventures are the research,” and “advisory is always tied to venture-building.”

The count is a consolidation dial: ~3 value-creating Engines, ~7 drawn as departments, ~13 raw work-types, ~28 in the prior agent list. The work points to 3.

Key takeaways
  • The Venture Engine (research-through-building), the Platform (≈ the Core), Knowledge & Ecosystem — over a Core and a 2-person founder Edge.
  • The “seven Domains” over-built: it split the one venture-research engine in two, and counted the Core and the Edge as departments.
  • Cross-check: the business plan’s own Year-1 team is ~3 functions + founders, never seven.
  • Theory route and work route converge on one shape — ~3 Engines + Core + Edge.

1. What FTLAB actually does (the work)

From the business plan, four permitted activities and four products/services:

Activity (BP §Nature of Activities)Product/Service (BP §Products & Services)Revenue
Research & Development(1) AI-Native Enterprise Framework — methodology + software (orchestration, memory, integration)Licensing ~70%
Innovation Incubation (each venture an SPV)(2) Venture Incubation Services — concept→market-validation, 18–24 mo, FTLAB takes equityEquity (long-term)
Strategic Consulting(3) Strategic Transformation Consulting — incumbents, 6–12 moConsulting ~30% (with Education)
IP Licensing(4) Executive Education & Thought Leadership — workshops, publications, eventsLead-gen + small fees

Market: GCC (UAE primary) — family offices, MNCs, sovereign entities, scale-ups. Team plan: 2–4 (Y1) → 4–10 (Y2) → 10–15 (Y3). Founders cover Interim CTO/CFO/Compliance/DPO in Year 1; first hires are Principal Solutions Architect, Senior Engineer, Operations Lead.

2. Two founder intentions that collapse the work

The activities look like four (or more) separate functions until you read the founders' intent:

  1. The ventures are the research (Q1: "the ventures are not a commercial byproduct of the research — they are the research methodology"; Q13: "the ventures are the research"; Design Principle #1 "Learning in Action"). → Research & Development and Innovation Incubation are one coupled activity, not two domains.
  2. Advisory is never standalone (Q7: "FTLAB's consulting/advisory work is always tied to venture building or investment — never purely advisory"). → Strategic Consulting is the same venture engine pointed at an incumbent (a co-venture/transformation), not a separate practice.

So FTLAB's core is a single learning-in-action venture engine, productised as a platform/IP it licenses, amplified by knowledge/education that builds credibility and generates leads. Everything else (capital, compliance, operations, governance) is founder-held.

3. The derivation — and the count at each level

The number of "roles" is a consolidation dial. Each level answers a different question; all are derived from the same work.

The work points to Level B (~3 Engines), not Level C (7). The seven-Domain model is a defensible consolidation of Level D, but measured against the founders' actual intent it over-constructs in two specific ways:

  • It split the one venture-research engine into two (D1 Research + D3 Venture Building) — directly against "the ventures are the research."
  • It counted the substrate and the governance layer as departments (D2 memory + D4 platform are infrastructure; D5 quality/guardianship + D7 capital/ops are founder-held governance) rather than as the Core and the Edge.

4. The three Engines, grounded in the work

  • The Venture Engine — originate theses, design/build/operate AI-born ventures (SPVs), and run incumbent transformations as co-ventures. Absorbs R&D + Incubation + Consulting. This is FTLAB's methodology and its long-term equity value. (BP §2; Q1, Q7, Q13.)
  • The Platform — build, license, support, and improve the AI-Native Enterprise Framework (agent orchestration, memory/knowledge systems, integration, blueprints). The ~70% licensing revenue. Note: this is largely the same artefact as the Core below — FTLAB's licensed product is the substrate its own ventures run on. (BP §Products 1, §Revenue Mix.)
  • Knowledge & Ecosystem — publications and thought-leadership (credibility + lead-gen, Q5), executive education, and the GCC/sovereign/MNC/partnership relationships (Q8, Q9). Absorbs Education + Marketing/Comms + Business Development. (BP §Products 4, §Market.)

The Core (substrate): the shared agent infrastructure, institutional memory, and orchestration all three Engines run on. For FTLAB this is largely the Platform product, turned inward.

The Edge (the only humans): the two founders holding the three Triumvirate functions — Shayan (Managing Partner: lead Intent-Setter + Capital/Finance + Operations + Compliance/DPO), Mehran (General Partner: research agenda + product architecture + partnerships + AI governance), both holding Guardian as a mutual check (to be externalised later).

5. The cross-check that matters: the business plan's own team

The business plan's near-term human team is not seven departments. Year 1 is Founders + 2 Engineers + Operations Manager + Business Development (~5 people). That maps cleanly onto:

  • Engineers → Platform / Core
  • Business Development → Knowledge & Ecosystem
  • Operations Manager → Edge/Core (ops)
  • Founders → Edge (Intent/Capital/Guardian) and the direction of the Venture Engine

FTLAB's own plan already describes ~3 working functions + a founder Edge — the same shape as Level B. The seven Domains never appear in the business plan; they were a framework elaboration.

6. Answering the question directly

  • Did I fit it into 7? Yes — the seven was inherited from the theory-driven pass and not re-derived. Fair challenge.
  • Would it be 10? No. Ten was the v0.2 theory elaboration. Re-derived from the work it goes the other way — fewer.
  • More or fewer? Fewer. ~3 value-creating Engines, a Core (≈ the Platform), and a 2-person founder Edge holding three governance functions.
  • Does this contradict the earlier Option 2 collapse? No — it confirms it from the other direction. The theory route (sorting the 7 into Core·Engines·Edge) and the work route (deriving from FTLAB's activities + founder intent) both land at ~3 Engines + Core + Edge. Two roads, one destination.

7. Implication for the determination

This strengthens the determination (ftlab-architecture-determination-book1-grounded-v0.1.md) and corrects its Engine count from an inherited "seven faces" to a derived ~3 Engines. It also sharpens which three: the Venture Engine (research-through-building), the Platform (≈ the Core), and Knowledge & Ecosystem — not the framework's D1/D3/D6 as separately drawn. Held, per the founders, as an evolving shape rather than a fixed chart: the number is a floor that earns the right to widen only as the work demands, never a template to fill.
