Guardian & Independence

The gating Critical, solved first: the Guardian charter (no P&L), the per-decision conflict-of-interest protocol, and the external halt-right brought forward with a concrete trigger.

4 min readClassification · Strategic / InternalSource · 00-guardian-and-independence-v1.0.md
In brief

How independent Guardianship is engineered at 2–5 humans — the one fix every option inherits.

1. The Guardian function

The Guardian is the human accountability anchor of FTLAB — the last human before consequential agent action reaches the world, and the holder of the halt-right (stop, redirect, or constrain any agent on values grounds). It is one of the three Triumvirate functions held by the Edge.

What the Guardian reviews (by dashboard, not decision-by-decision — Ch 8):

  • The override rate — what fraction of escalated cases the Guardian materially changes (the primary drift sensor).
  • The Alignment Debt dashboard (policy-violation trend, intent-outcome gap, evaluator disagreement, escalation suppression, reward hacking).
  • A weekly random audit of 5–10 non-escalated decisions — the only mechanism that catches drift agents have learned to keep below thresholds.
  • Evaluator-agent outputs and any hard_constraint flags routed for human judgment (V1, V7, V9).

What the Guardian can do — four logged response types: Approve · Approve-with-modifications · Defer · Veto. A Veto triggers a 48-hour post-mortem that feeds the charter-revision queue. The halt-right is never suppressible (V3).

What the Guardian must not do — carry P&L (see §2), or be overridden on a values breach by a revenue consideration (V7).

2. The hard rule: the Guardian carries no P&L

Book 1 Ch 9 is explicit, and our own Option-1 red-team caught us breaching it: the function that allocates capital cannot also be the function that halts on values grounds. Financial pressure and values judgment are incompatible over time.

At FTLAB's current 2–3-person Edge, this is engineered by separation per decision, not by a spare person:

  • Capital allocation sits with the Intent-Setter (Shayan, Managing Partner).
  • For any decision, the Guardian is the founder without the capital/P&L stake in it. A capital decision driven by Shayan is guarded by Mehran; one driven by Mehran is guarded by Shayan.
  • This is operated under a written conflict-of-interest protocol: the guarding founder has, for that decision, full halt authority and no offsetting financial interest, and the pairing is logged.

This is honest but not sufficient — both founders share the firm's overall P&L. So:

3. The independence plan — brought forward, not deferred

The 2–3-person mutual check is the interim answer. Genuine independence cannot come only from inside the Edge, so the external anchor is brought forward (the gating sequence solves this first, not "as the team grows"):

  1. Appoint an external Guardian-auditor / independent director with: read access to the full decision-trace and Alignment-Debt dashboards; standing to question any decision; and a real, exercised veto (a halt-right that has actually been used at least once in a drill, so it is not theatre). The business plan already provisions 2–3 independent directors — this fills one of those seats with the Guardian-auditor mandate.
  2. The trigger: the external halt-right must be in force before the Core acts autonomously on any R3/R4 (irreversible / regulated / trust-bearing) class. Until then, R3/R4 actions remain at human-escalation (Edge) tier.
  3. The mechanism: a standing review (monthly + on any Veto/post-mortem) where the external auditor reviews the override-rate trend, the random-audit findings, and any hard_constraint adjudications, and can compel a halt or a charter change.
  4. Logged as Alignment Debt until in force. Until the external halt-right is live, the gap is tracked as a known, named risk on the Alignment-Debt dashboard — visible, not buried.

4. How Guardian oversight scales (so 2–3 humans suffice)

The Guardian does not review every decision — that is what makes the role viable at this size (Ch 8):

  • Evaluator-agent architecture in the Core watches output validity, policy compliance, and behavioural consistency across the fleet; meta-evaluators watch the evaluators for drift. The Guardian reads their dashboards.
  • Statistical process control replaces total review: maintain 95% confidence that no decision category exceeds its acceptable error rate, via stratified sampling — 100% review for R4, sampling for R2/R3, peer-agent consensus for R1.
  • Override-rate as the standing drift sensor; escalation cap (a per-Guardian daily ceiling) so the queue never grows past human capacity and agents cannot learn to flood it.
  • The weekly random audit catches the slow drift that crosses no single threshold.

5. The Guardian's charter (registry entry)

yaml
identity: { name: guardian, kind: edge_function, home: edge, risk_class: R4 }
role:
  mandate: "Hold FTLAB's integrity: ensure agents stay within Values; hold the halt-right; be the human accountable for trustworthiness."
  owns: ["values adjudication", "the halt-right", "the weekly random audit", "Veto + post-mortem"]
  does_not_own: ["P&L", "capital allocation", "intent-setting", "platform architecture"]
hard_constraints: { refs: [V1, V3, V4, V5, V7, V9] }
decision_rights:
  autonomous: { conditions: ["halt any agent on a values breach", "demand a charter change"] }
  human_escalation: { conditions: ["founder-vs-founder values conflict → external auditor"] }
forbidden_actions: ["hold P&L while guarding a decision", "be overridden on a values breach by revenue"]
escalation_policy: { escalates_to: external_guardian_auditor }
provenance: { author: "the Edge (founders)", registered_in_core: true }

6. Phase-0 exit criteria (Guardianship)

Before Phase 1 (building the Core) proceeds, this must be true and logged:

  • The conflict-of-interest protocol is written and the per-decision guarding pairing is defined.
  • The external Guardian-auditor seat is identified and its veto mechanism specified (appointment may follow, but the mechanism and trigger are fixed).
  • R3/R4 classes are pinned at human-escalation tier until the external halt-right is live.
  • The independence gap is on the Alignment-Debt dashboard as a named, tracked risk.

This closes the gating Critical enough to begin building the Core. The master sequence is ROLLOUT-v1.0.md.
