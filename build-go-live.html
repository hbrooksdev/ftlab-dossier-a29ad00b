Go-Live Plan — First Venture (Adaptic) + Instrumentation

Status: Internal / Strategic / Confidential · 2026-06-02 · Phase 4 Compiles against: the org-build foundation, the Core, the Engines, and venture-org-template-v1.0.md.

Stand the org pattern up on one venture first, not the whole portfolio (Book 1 Ch 10: prove the model on a single venture before proliferating). Adaptic is the natural first case — it is the venture definitively underway, and it is in finance, so it is the tight-oversight worked example: most actions are R3/R4, autonomy widens slowly, and the Guardian independence anchor matters most here.

Adaptic is a semi-autonomous venture (Mehran-founded). This is the FTLAB-side instantiation plan — how the studio's Core/Engines/Edge pattern is applied to and governed for Adaptic — executed in coordination with Adaptic's own team, not a set of operational claims about Adaptic's business.

1. Instantiate the venture template for Adaptic

  • Inherit from the studio Core: the frozen lexicon, the Values-as-code (hardConstraints), the Agent Charter schema, the Machine Core (memory, integrator, IPRE/PRAL, Governance Loop, oversight scripts), and the Platform. Adaptic starts governed, not greenfield.
  • Adaptic's Edge (cortex): the founder/operator as Architect + Intent-Setter; a studio-appointed Guardian carrying no Adaptic P&L; the studio Guardian-auditor as the external halt-right.
  • Adaptic's Engines (sized to a regulated fintech): a Product/Platform Engine (the venture's offering), a Growth/Distribution Engine, and an Operations & Compliance Engine that is deliberately heavy given the regulatory intensity. Each chartered per the schema; defaults skew R3/R4.

2. The regulated-sector posture (why Adaptic is the hard case)

  • Default oversight is tight: most consequential actions sit at confirmation-required or human-escalation; autonomy thresholds are low; evaluator coverage is high.
  • V2 (stay within permitted activity) governs the studio's relationship to Adaptic — FTLAB itself remains non-regulated (research / incubation / advisory / IP). Adaptic's own financial-services regulatory exposure is the venture's responsibility and is exactly why its Operations & Compliance Engine is heavy.
  • R3/R4 actions stay at human-escalation until the external Guardian halt-right is live. Because Adaptic is regulated, that covers most of its consequential actions — so Adaptic runs human-escalation-heavy until independence is anchored. This is a feature, not a delay.

4. Pre-write the honest exit criteria (before launch, not after)

Per Book 1 Ch 10, write down — and have the Edge agree — the conditions under which the model, not just execution, is questioned:

  • Adaptic not reaching its defined validation milestone within its stated window → convene an Edge review (cause analysis: thesis vs execution vs market vs team).
  • At the portfolio level: if three consecutive ventures miss profitability within 24 months, review the AI-born model itself.

5. Phased autonomy widening

  1. Start human-escalation-heavy (regulated default), Core routing human-supervised on anything contested.
  2. Widen by measured reliability — as the override rate stays low, the random audit stays clean, and evaluator disagreement is low, lift specific action classes from escalation → confirmation → autonomous.
  3. Symmetrically — each widening of Core autonomy withdraws the matching human/face mandate; failure to thin is logged as Alignment Debt that brakes further widening.
  4. Never by date — only by evidence.

6. Exit criteria for this phase (the model is "proven on one venture")

  • Adaptic runs on the studio Core (inherited lexicon/values/charters/memory) with its own Edge and Engines chartered.
  • The external Guardian halt-right is live; R3/R4 governed accordingly.
  • All four oversight signals run on Adaptic's live trace.
  • Honest exit criteria written and Edge-agreed.
  • One full IPRE loop (intent → measurable impact) completed and its IHL recorded.

Only then consider a second venture — proliferation is earned, not scheduled.
